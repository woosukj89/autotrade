## Why Our Strategies Failed

### Problem 1: We Never Bought the Winners

Our DCF model likely NEVER flagged Apple as a buy because:
- High P/E ratio (25-35x) exceeds our "value" thresholds
- Our model caps growth at 5-12%, Apple was growing faster
- We required "margin of safety" that Apple never offered

**Apple's financials in 2016:**
- P/E: ~12x (actually looked cheap!)
- ROIC: 25%+
- Growing services revenue at 20%+
- Massive buyback program

Our model should have caught this, but likely didn't because:
- Our universe selection may have excluded it
- Growth cap was too conservative
- We sold too quickly when price rose

### Problem 2: We Sold Too Early

Our strategies sell when:
- Price reaches fair value (Original)
- Price exceeds intrinsic by 30% (Buy & Hold)
- Fundamentals "deteriorate" (True Buffett)

Buffett's approach:
- NEVER sells a great business just because it's "fairly valued"
- Only sells if the business model is broken
- Held Coca-Cola for 35+ years despite never being "cheap"

### Problem 3: Wrong Universe

Our model focuses on companies with:
- 5-7+ years of data (excludes newer growth companies)
- Traditional value metrics (misses quality growth)
- Quantifiable moats (can't measure brand power)

Buffett focuses on:
- Brand power (Coca-Cola, Apple, See's Candies)
- Network effects (American Express, Visa)
- Irreplaceable businesses
- Management quality (qualitative)

### Problem 4: Market Regime Strategy Failed Catastrophically

The -99.7% return on Market Regime suggests:
- The strategy likely went to cash and stayed there
- Missed the 2020-2021 recovery
- Possibly had bugs in regime detection

## What Buffett Did That We Can't Replicate

1. **Qualitative Analysis**
   - "I want businesses that even an idiot can run, because eventually one will"
   - Brand durability, management integrity, customer loyalty
   - None of this is in financial statements

2. **Special Deals**
   - Goldman Sachs preferred stock (2008): 10% dividend + warrants
   - Bank of America warrants (2011): Massive leverage
   - We can only buy common stock at market prices

3. **Information Advantage**
   - Direct conversations with CEOs
   - Decades of industry knowledge
   - Network of informed sources

4. **Scale Advantages**
   - Can buy entire companies (BNSF, Precision Castparts)
   - Insurance float provides free capital
   - Can negotiate favorable terms

5. **Psychological Discipline**
   - 60+ years of experience
   - Immune to FOMO and panic
   - Truly long-term thinking (decades, not quarters)

## Recommendations for Improvement

### 1. Remove Growth Cap / Raise Significantly
- Our 5-12% growth cap excludes the best compounders
- Apple, Microsoft, Google all grow faster
- Allow 15-20% growth for high-ROIC companies

### 2. Never Sell Quality
- If ROIC > 15% and moat is intact, NEVER sell based on price
- Only sell if business fundamentally deteriorates
- This is the biggest behavioral change needed

### 3. Concentrate More
- Buffett's top 5 = 70% of portfolio
- Our strategies limit to 30-40% per position
- Higher conviction = higher returns (and risk)

### 4. Include Apple/Tech
- Our model should have caught Apple in 2016
- Check if Apple was even in our universe
- Verify our ROIC calculation is correct

### 5. Longer Time Horizon
- Rebalance annually at most
- Better: Only when fundamentals change
- Best: Hold forever unless broken

### 6. Add Qualitative Screen
- Filter for strong brands
- Filter for recurring revenue
- Filter for high customer retention
- These predict moat better than ROIC alone

## The Hard Truth

**Quantitative value investing has underperformed for 15 years.**

The period 2014-2024 was historically bad for value:
- Low interest rates favored growth stocks
- Tech giants achieved unprecedented scale
- Traditional value metrics (P/E, P/B) were poor predictors
- Even Buffett admits value investing has gotten harder

Our model does what traditional quant value does - and traditional quant value lost.

To beat the market, we would need:
1. Better qualitative analysis (can't automate)
2. Longer holding periods (behavioral discipline)
3. Concentrated bets on highest conviction ideas
4. Willingness to pay fair price for great companies
5. Cash deployment during crashes (timing skill)

**Or simply: Buy SPY and hold.**
